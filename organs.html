Organs of respiratory system

The nose

Pyramid-shaped structue composed of bone and cartilage.

The choanae are lined with mucus, this substance is essential for humidifyng the air and trapping microbes and dust particles.

Have cilia, tiny hairs that contain and expel particles, thus filtering the air we breath.

Is hypervascularised, it has many blood capillaries that warm the air as it passes through.

The Mouth and the Pharynx

The mouth is a cavity in which there is a connective tissue covered with saliva and mucous, this is the place where the pharynx begins.

The pharynx is considered a hybrid organ as it is part of the respiratory systemand digestive system.

In addition to functioning as an air passageway, the pharynx also serves as a sounding board in the respiratory system.

The Larynx

The larynx is the organ where the voice is produced, is a short duct that
connects the pharynx to the trachea and comprises nine cartilages, of which three main ones stand out:

1. Thyroid cartilage: Better known as AdamÂ´s Apple: Develops due to the action of hormoness during male puberty.

2. The epiglottis: It is responsible for isolating fluid and foreign particles from the respiratory tract.

3. The cricoid cartilage: Connects the larynx to the trachea and protects the vocal cords responsible for voice production.

The Trachea

The trachea is a flexible tube, that measure 18 to 20 cm in an adult.

It is linedinternally by mucous cilia, which prevent particles and foreign matter from passing into the lungs.

Inside the trachea is the cough centre, where coughing is triggered as a reflex to expel any foreign agent from the body.

The Bronchi and Bronchioles

The trachea divides into two large branches know as a bronchi.

Bronchi are tubes about 1 cm in diameter at their widest point and divided into bronchioles.

The bronchioles, in turn, are subided into microscopic alveoli.

The subdivision from the trachea to the bronchioles is know as the bronchial tree.

The Alveoli

Are the branching of the broncholies.

Are 150 million alveoli in each lung, approximately.

Provides a large surface area for gas exchange.

Are grouped into alveolar sacs.

The gas exchange occurs here.

The Lungs

The lungs are soft, spongy and easily expandable organs.

Each lung is convered with a pleura membrane, lubricant covering between the lungs and the rib cage during inhalation and exhalation.

They can store more than one and half liters of air.

The Diaphragm

Located below the lungs. Is the primary muscle of respiratory and most of the time is involuntarily.

Is a dome-shaped muscle that contracts rhythmically and continuously.

Decreasing the volume of the chest inhalation and increasing it during exhalation.
